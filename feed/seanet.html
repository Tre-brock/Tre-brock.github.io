Seamonkey internet suite review (Fri May 3 2024)

The Seamonkey internet suite includes multiple programs in one. These are; browser, HTML editor, contact book, mail and RSS client, and a calendar. It uses the gecko rendering engine by Mozilla (also used by Firefox and Thunderbird). Seamonkey has a good ui, it has the different apps at the bottom of the browser and the bookmarks tabs and controls at the top. My one issue is that to close tabs you have to either right click a tab to close it or press the tab close button while you are on the tab you want to close. To fix this issue you can install. Seatab X 2 from the Seamonkey addon store. The Seamonkey browser is light on ram and is pretty fast. The mail client is smooth. I love how you can have many accounts at once and look through them all. Having your RSS feed with your mail is convenient too. Seamonkey also has a feature where it can detect RSS on a page. The HTML editor is good. It lets you resize images easily. You can edit the HTML page as if you are viewing it and not viewing code. It great to see it visually. But not being able to edit CSS is a bad spot, meaning you will need more then 1 editor. The IRC client of Seamonkey is actually the Firefox addons ChatZilla, and it works. Although it is an addon it feels like it was built for Seamonkey. The ui is basic but is fits Seamonkey. It works for being an IRC client. It's speedy and clean. It also supports plugins itself and integrates into the Seamonkey settings. Finally I will be reviewing the calendar. It is built into the mail app, you get to it by clicking "Events and Tasks" at the top bar then clicking calendar. It's a pretty basic calendar app. It lets you use local calendars and use ones from the internet. It also lets you create tasks. It does what you think a calendar would do. It is also technically an addon called lightning. Overall Seamonkey is a great internet suite that I recommend you use.

Ps: if you want a good ad and tracker blocker the look up ublock origin legacy and install that from GitHub.
